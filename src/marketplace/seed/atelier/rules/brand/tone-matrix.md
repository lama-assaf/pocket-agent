# tone matrix

tone shifts with context, voice does not.

## the axes

most products operate on two axes:

- **register**: casual to formal
- **energy**: warm to clinical

map common surfaces onto the matrix and decide where each lives. examples:

- homepage hero: warm + casual
- onboarding: warm + clear
- error messages: clear + clinical (don't be cute in errors)
- legal terms: formal + clinical
- support replies: warm + clear
- celebration moments (milestones, successes): warm + casual + energetic

## what tone does

- adjusts vocabulary (more / less colloquial)
- adjusts contractions (we'll vs we will)
- adjusts pacing (short sentences vs longer ones)
- adjusts use of humor

## what tone never does

- changes the brand's principles
- introduces vocabulary that belongs to a different audience
- breaks consistency within a single surface

## test

read three pieces of copy from different surfaces side by side. they should sound like the same brand. one is warm and casual; one is clear and clinical; both feel like the same company.
