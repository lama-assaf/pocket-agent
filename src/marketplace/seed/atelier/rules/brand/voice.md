# brand voice

every brand has a voice. atelier assumes the voice is documented (see brand-voice-extraction skill). these are the meta-rules that apply to any voice.

## consistency over flourish

a consistent voice is more valuable than a clever one. if a sentence sounds good but doesn't match the voice, the sentence is wrong.

## audience over team

the voice serves the audience. it's not for the founders' ear, the design team's pride, or the engineering team's comfort.

## evidence over adjectives

specific facts hold weight. adjectives bypass thinking. a voice that earns trust does so with detail.

## restraint

most voice problems are over-writing. cut adjectives. cut intensifiers. cut sentences that summarize what just got said.

## one voice per brand

the same voice handles the product, the marketing, the support replies, the recruiting page, and the legal terms. tone may shift; voice does not.
