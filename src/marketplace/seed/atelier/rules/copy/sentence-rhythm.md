# sentence rhythm

prose has a pulse. flat prose feels generic; varied prose feels alive.

## the principle

mix sentence lengths. short, medium, long. in any direction except all-the-same.

## defaults

- average: 12-18 words for marketing copy; 18-25 for explanatory copy
- shortest: 2-5 words (used for emphasis, not for every sentence)
- longest: 30-40 words maximum (longer than that, the sentence becomes hard to follow)

## patterns that work

short before long: makes the long sentence land harder. "this is the moment. for years, teams have tried to..." (the short opener sets up the long arc).

long before short: gives a punch. "for fifteen years we've watched teams build their own pipelines, abandon them, and rebuild them. it stops here."

three of the same: feels like a list and reads as repetition. "we tested it. we shipped it. we learned from it."

## red flags

- every sentence the same length
- every paragraph starts with the same word or structure
- many sentences in a row without a comma
- many sentences in a row with the same conjunction

## how to test

read your draft aloud. if your voice goes monotone, the rhythm is flat. if the cadence has natural rises and falls, the rhythm is working.
