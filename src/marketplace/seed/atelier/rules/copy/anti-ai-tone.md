# anti-ai tone

ai writing has tells. avoid them.

## the most obvious tells

- "delve", "delving"
- "navigate the complexities"
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "the realm of"
- "tapestry"
- "foster" / "fostering" (as a verb, not the noun)
- "leverage" used as a verb
- "robust", "seamless", "cutting-edge", "groundbreaking"

## structural tells

- every paragraph starting with "moreover", "furthermore", "additionally"
- conclusions that summarize what was just said
- bulleted lists where prose would be clearer
- bullets that are all the same length
- bullets that are all complete sentences ending in periods
- "let's break it down" or "here's the thing" used as filler

## tonal tells

- excessive hedging ("it's worth noting", "it's important to remember")
- excessive emphasis ("absolutely", "incredibly", "truly")
- unearned warmth ("we're so excited to share")
- meta-commentary on what's being written ("in this article we'll explore")

## what reads as human

- sentences of varying length
- specifics over abstractions
- one good adjective, used sparingly
- direct claims without surrounding hedge
- references to actual situations or actual people
- the writer's reasoning visible, not hidden behind passive constructions
- the willingness to say something less polished if it's true

## the test

if your draft sounds like every other marketing blog post, the ai tone is showing. rewrite the openings and the transitions. those are the spots where ai tone hides.
