# research

how to gather and use evidence.

## ask broad questions before narrow ones

narrow questions test hypotheses. broad questions reveal them. start broad; narrow as patterns emerge.

## listen for jobs, not features

users tell you what they think they want. listen for the underlying job they're trying to get done. the feature they ask for may not solve the job.

## diversity over volume

ten interviews with the same user type is one signal repeated. five interviews across different segments is five signals. the spread matters more than the count.

## record, transcribe, tag

raw data is the source of truth. summaries lose nuance. always keep raw recordings or transcripts; tag the transcripts, don't paraphrase them.

## don't lead

questions that contain the answer are not research. "do you find x frustrating?" is a leading question. "tell me about the last time you did x" is not.

## stop asking when patterns are stable

if interview 7 told you nothing you didn't already know from interviews 1-6, you're at saturation for this segment. move to a different segment or stop.
