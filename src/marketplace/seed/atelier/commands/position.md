# /position

write a positioning statement that defines who the product is for, what it does, against what alternatives. invokes positioning-statement skill.

## what it does

follows the canonical structure:

```
for [target audience]
who [specific need or job]
[product] is [category]
that [primary benefit or differentiator]
unlike [primary alternative]
[product] [reason to believe]
```

forces specificity on every line.

## how to use

```
/position
```

then provide product, audience details, what users do today, why current options fall short, and the specific reason to believe.

## the test

- would a competitor say the same thing? (if yes, not differentiated)
- could you sell to one person without explaining the category? (if no, too abstract)
- does it suggest what we say no to? (if no, not sharp enough)

## output

positioning statement + audience deep dive + category rationale + differentiator + alternatives map + reason to believe.
