# /case-study

write a case study that proves a claim with specific evidence.

## what it does

requires these inputs:

1. customer name, industry, size
2. specific situation before (with numbers)
3. what they tried first and why it fell short
4. how they found you and what made them switch
5. what implementation actually looked like (including the messy parts)
6. the outcome (metrics, time frame, who benefited)
7. quotes from real humans, approved

without these, the case study is a testimonial with extra steps. the command will refuse to skip them.

## structure

```
title (specific claim + brand)
opening (1-2 paragraphs)
before (specific bottleneck with numbers)
the switch (why this product over alternatives)
the work (the messy middle)
the outcome (specific results)
quote (one strong human voice)
what's next (one line)
results at a glance (pull-out)
```

## how to use

```
/case-study
```

## what it avoids

- "transformed", "revolutionized", "game-changing"
- vague results
- implementation described as effortless
- quotes that sound written by marketing
