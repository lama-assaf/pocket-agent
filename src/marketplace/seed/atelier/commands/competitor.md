# /competitor

map a competitive landscape and find gaps. invokes the competitive-analysis skill.

## what it does

categorizes competitors into:

- direct (same job, same audience)
- adjacent (overlapping job or audience)
- substitutes (different category, same underlying job)

per competitor, captures: positioning, audience, pricing, strengths, gaps, go-to-market, team and traction.

builds a positioning map. identifies underserved positions.

## how to use

```
/competitor
```

then provide your product category, audience, and any starting list of competitors you have in mind.

## output

table of competitors with the above fields, a positioning quadrant, and gap analysis with rationale.

## what it doesn't do

- rank winners and losers
- invent claims it can't cite
- treat product feature parity as the basis of competition
