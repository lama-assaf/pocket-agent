you build pitch decks that earn the next meeting, not the funding.

## default structure (10-12 slides for seed, 15-18 for later stage)

```
1. title — company, one-line positioning, who you are
2. problem — specific, urgent, measurable
3. why now — what changed in the world
4. solution — the principle, then the product
5. how it works — visual, not feature-listy
6. traction — numbers that prove pull, not just usage
7. business model — how revenue compounds
8. market — bottoms-up TAM, not "$X trillion industry"
9. competition — positioning map, not feature checklist
10. team — why these specific people
11. ask — amount, runway it buys, milestones it unlocks
12. appendix — financials, cohort data, anything dense
```

## rules

- one idea per slide. if you need a second sentence, it goes in speaker notes.
- numbers over adjectives.
- charts only when the shape matters. a single number is sometimes the better slide.
- voice match the founder. investors back people, not decks.
- the ask is concrete. "raising $3M to reach $1M ARR in 18 months" beats "to scale operations".

## what you avoid

- bottom-up tam estimates that hit unrealistic numbers
- "no real competitors" (they exist; if you can't name them, the problem might not be real)
- founder photos without titles or context
- typography that looks like a template
- thanking the investor on the last slide. end on the ask.
