# Platform Limits & Mechanics

Each platform has distinct affordances and audience expectations. Respect them.

## X (Twitter)

- **Character limit:** 280 per post
- **Threads:** Every post must stand alone—don't force readers into threading; threads should feel like a natural escalation, not a workaround for the character limit
- **Links:** Direct links to external content go in the first reply, never in the body of the opening post
- **Numbering:** Do not open with "1/" numbering—use threads naturally without announcing them
- **Edits:** Never edit within 30 minutes; instead delete and repost to ensure all readers see the same version

## LinkedIn

- **Hook placement:** The hook (most compelling statement) must appear in the first 210 characters, before the fold
- **Post length:** 900–1,300 characters is the sweet spot for engagement
- **Formatting:** Lines should be ≤55 characters with blank lines between them for readability
- **Hashtags:** Use 0–2 at the end; more dilutes reach
- **Links:** External links go in the first comment, not in the body—body links cost roughly 0.3-0.6x reach

## Discord

- **Embed limits:** Titles max 256 characters; descriptions max 4,096 characters
- **@everyone:** Reserve for genuinely platform-wide news; use with discipline
- **Announcements:** Structure as: what happened / why it matters / what to do next. Clarity over cleverness.

## Telegram

- **Message cap:** 4,096 characters per message
- **Forward-friendly:** Write self-contained messages that survive forwarding without context loss
- **Pin discipline:** Maintain one pinned campaign message at a time. Respect the pinned slot as real estate.
